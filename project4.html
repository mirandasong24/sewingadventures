SUMMER SUN TOP

What did this piece used to look like?

This short-sleeved button-down shirt was a men’s large in a creamy white color with a light yellow tint.

How did I go about upcycling this piece?

To fit the color palette of the pieces that I had upcycled so far, I selected this light-colored shirt and transformed it from a formal men’s button-down to an informal women’s collared top. When I first put on the shirt, it was very long - including the sleeves! To fix the lengths, I folded over the short sleeves twice and ironed them before hemming the edges to create a more feminine clothing style. After taking in the sleeves, I then needed to crop the shirt and fit the waist. To that end, I hand-sewed the bottom of the sides of the shirt to the insides of the sleeves, creating a template for where I should take in the rest of the shirt. After some more hand-sewing and machine magic, this piece fit me much better than when I had laid my eyes on it!
