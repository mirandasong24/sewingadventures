CHERRY BLOSSOM DRESS

What did this piece used to look like?

Originally, it was a size 16 in girls dress that had a crossover top attached with red and pink straps. It also had streamers on the sides to tie a bow in the back and fit the dress to the body.

How did I go about upcycling this piece?

I cut off the crossover top as well as the side streamers, thus shortening the dress by pulling the skirt up to my chest. I then sewed on the cut-off side streamers as the two new straps of the dress. At that point, I had a dress in the simplest sense, but I still wanted to highlight the body figure. To that end, I decided to flip the crossover top upside-down and sew it to the middle of the dress, with the previous straps of the original still attached. These straps were now used to tie a bow in the back to fit the dress to the body. And voila, the cherry-blossom dress!
